MAKERLABS

MakerLabs is a makerspace in Vancouver
that provides you with the tools, space, and skills to make almost anything.

OVERVIEW

The goal of this project was to work with a business, in this case MakerLabs, to find a friction points through the us of cultural probes and interviews. Upon discovering problems areas within the business, we were to come up with a solution. Throughout the project my main focus was filming, website interface development, research, and ideating cultural probes.

HOME

The target for the homepage was to simplify information into the four main sections which the website could be broken down into, to help guide users.

EDUCATION

A calender system was implemented to allow for quick access to upcoming events, with interactive elements to display course names and staff. While maintaining options such as private courses to drive income.

MEMBERSHIP & STUDIO

Since MakerLabs houses many small business, we developed an interactive map, which would showcase where the business is located within the space. This included a registration form when space was available.

ORDER

In order to prevent large email threads an interactive step-by-step system was used to help users. This included a guide on how submissions where to be made making use of images, and examples.

PROBLEM

Our initial process in doing so involved the observation of the space, learning about the business workflow, and model. After which, we conducted interviews with both employees and users of the space to explore potential problems areas. In doing so, we noticed the business relied heavily on their website for providing information to both new and old customers. However, the content and layout were not intuitive and difficult to navigate due to important content being hidden behind various pages.

PROCESS

In order to learn more about the problem and possible ways in which we could solve it, we conducted interviews within each branch of the company. This included the CEO, Managers, Instructors, and the various independent makers within the space, and customers. Despite, everyone having much to say about the space, and what they like or dislike, we found a few common problems:

Before coming up with the solution, we started by using cultural probes to get feedback on what the key people in the company felt was a problem. In addition to the websites information heirarchy. Upon the deployment and completion of the probes. We found the biggest issue, and one which would help the business the most and touch on each aspect was the re-arranging of the information heirachy on the website through the development of a more UX friendly experience.

CULTURAL PROBES

We want to help MakerLabs improve the way in which they communicate their identity and services topotential customers as well as how they handle customer inquiries.The MakerLabs team wants to invest more resources into the growth of the company, however, they spend a large portion of their time responding to emails and providing answers that are already on the website.

Describe MakerLabs

Purpose: To figure out what word best describes MakerLabs.

Results: The chosen words were: Polyglot, Autodidact, Awesome and inspiring, overwhelming, empowering, detail-oriented, ambitious, demanding, complex, multi-faceted

Findings:The words that were selected were complex and encompasses several different meanings, which reflected the diversity of MakerLabs. One participant, Mel, even included a list of supporting words to describe the different aspects of MakerLabs.

Poloroids

Purpose: To gain insight into our participants’ personal experiences with MakerLabs by responding artistically to four statements. They also reflected on how they feel about their responses through the use of emoticons.

Findings: We learned that all of our participants’ favourite aspect of MakerLabs is the opportunity to work and connect with other makers. In addition, they share the common goal of expanding their knowledge and working hard to help increase revenue for MakerLabs.

Cards

Purpose: To get a better understanding of their frustrations, types of customer inquiries (both in person and over e-mail), and what they spend most of their time on and where would they better invest it.

Findings: We found that our participants spent 3 hours a day responding to e-mails regarding quotes for outsourced projects. If they didn’t spend so much time on e-mails and training, they would improve the website, plan for events, and complete fabrication orders. In addition, many people who visit MakerLabs do not know about the types of services they provide, despite these details being outlined on the website.

Bottles

Purpose: To understand their levels of productivity each day and what the factors were behind it.

Findings: Between Tuesday and Sunday, productivity levels were low to adequate, and were mainly affected by customer interruptions. This includes providing assistance with tools, member files, and general inquiries. In addition, the laser cutter is shared by both customers and employees, which causes delays for outsourced projects.

SOLUTION

Thus, we began looking at the websites information hierarchy and how to improve it. This process involved an analysis of both the current website and other websites belonging to competitors. In doing so we began interviewing employees one on one to get a stronger understanding of their needs and where friction points lied within the website and how this translated into the physical work environment. After doing so, we implemented cultural probes which were presented within a box to obtain more information through an abstract method. What we found was that the MakerLabs team wanted to invest more resources into the growth of the company, however, they spend a large portion of their time responding to emails and providing answer that already exist on the website. Through our research we found a direction in which to focus on when building the website for the company.
